⚑ Golden rules — read before every call

The discipline matters more than the questions. Break these and the data is noise.

  1. Past & specific, never hypothetical. "Tell me about the last time you watched" — not "would you watch more if…". Real memories are data; future opinions are noise.
  2. Don't pitch, don't lead, don't sell. The moment you explain or defend a feature, the answer is contaminated. Be a curious journalist.
  3. Let silence work. After they answer, wait. The second sentence is the honest one.
  4. Dig on emotion & effort. When they light up ("bachche khush") or mention work they did (told a friend, removed cable), stop and ask "why" 2–3 times.
  5. Anchor every claim to a moment. "I watch daily" → "When exactly? What time? Who was in the room? What did you put on first?"
  6. Write channel & show names verbatim. Exact titles are the strongest signal of the job.
