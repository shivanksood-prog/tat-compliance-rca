1. The Gap — 2,243 Tickets

The overall TAT dropped from 77.4% to 73.1%. What does that mean in absolute tickets?

The gap is 2,243 tickets. If 2,243 more tickets had been resolved within TAT in the recent period, we would match the Dec baseline of 77.4%. The question is: which partners failed to close these 2,243 tickets?

How we measure partner impact (New Definition)

We use a rate-focused metric instead of the traditional weight×rate decomposition:

Delta Tickets = (Dec TAT% − Rec TAT%) × Recent Tickets

This answers: "If this partner had maintained their own December performance, how many more tickets would be within TAT?"
We use this because LL bucket weight is stable (42.7% → 41.2%) — the drop is driven by rate degradation, not volume shifts. The old contribution formula conflated weight changes with rate changes, ranking partners who simply had fewer tickets above those whose TAT actually collapsed.
